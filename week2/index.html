# Training a GAN

This week we will be training a GAN, stylegan2-ada-pytorch, to generate images of Pokemons. I know nothing about Pokemon, but I wanted a fairly large dataset of small images and eventually came across a website with Pokemon sprites. These sprites are all pixel images of various Pokemon, and the images range in size from 64px to 96px. Using Photoshop batch processing, I filled in the backgrounds with white, upscaled to 256px, and converted to RGB. With all images now in the same format, stylegan2-ada-pytorch can create a dataset and train on them. Files from Pokedex (opens new window)

# Try Training Pokemon

In this notebook, we set up StyleGAN (the newest version, StyleGAN2-ada-pytorch), copy over an image dataset of Pokemon, prepare a dataset for StyleGAN, and then train StyleGAN.

You only need to train for as long as you feel like, one or two hours is fine. We just want to get experience running StyleGAN and look at the progress images to see how the generator develops over time.
